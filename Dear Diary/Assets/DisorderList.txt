Disorder: Depression
Description: A mood disorder causing persistent feelings of sadness or disinterest. Also commonly referred to as Major Depressive Disorder or Clinical Depression. It may alter the way you think and behave, leading to a variety of emotional and physical problems. 
Symptoms: Concentration issues -- Feelings of worthlessness and/or pessimism -- Eating too little or too much -- Sleeping too little or too much -- Loss on interest in activities -- Feeling irritable -- Suicidal Thoughts
Tips: Eat healthy, well balanced meals -- Limit alcohol and drug use -- Get enough sleep -- Exercise for 30 minutes daily -- If you are on a medicine, do not stop taking it without a doctor’s permission -- If you feel as if you need help, reach out to a therapist or tell your doctor about your issues -- If you are thinking about harming yourself, please call the National Suicide Hotline at 1-800-273-8255 -- If you feel as if you need help, reach out to a therapist or tell your doctor about your issues -- Have a daily routine to ensure productivity -- Do not lie in bed during the day or allow yourself to take naps

Disorder: Anxiety
Description: Anxiety is a condition of persistent and uncontrollable nervousness, stress, and worry that is triggered by anticipation of future events, memories of past events, or ruminations over day-to-day events, both trivial and major, with disproportionate fears of catastrophic consequences. 
Symptoms: Sudden feelings of danger, panic, or dread -- Rapid breathing or hyperventilation -- A severe inability to focus -- Feeling extremely hot or cold suddenly -- Having chest pains / tightness -- Sweating excessively --
Generally feeling nervous and restless in your everyday life -- Suffering from insomnia -- Being very particular to the point it may be considered obsessive (may be indicative of OCD as well) -- Suffering from a particular life event or experience that has occured in the past (may be indicative of PTSD)
Tips: Eat healthy, well balanced meals -- Limit alcohol and drug use -- Get enough sleep -- Exercise for 30 minutes daily -- If you are on a medicine, do not stop taking it without a doctor’s permission -- If you feel as if you need help, reach out to a therapist or tell your doctor about your issues -- Limit caffiene use -- When having a panic attack, count to 10 slowly and / or take deep breaths  -- Try to identify what triggers your anxiety. If possible, face these issues head on, but if not, try to avoid these triggers.

Disorder: OCD
Description: a mental disorder in which people have unwanted and repeated thoughts, feelings, ideas, sensations (obsessions), and behaviors that drive them to do something over and over (compulsions). Often the person carries out the behaviors to get rid of the obsessive thoughts. 
Symptoms: Having anxiety about germs and dirt, having an obsession with cleaning -- Needing for perfect symmetry and order -- Feelings that you must follow particular practices or routines or else you or others will be harmed in some way, even if they are not life threatening -- Consistently having disturbing thoughts or images about yourself
Tips: Eat healthy, well balanced meals -- Limit alcohol and drug use -- Get enough sleep -- Exercise for 30 minutes daily -- If you are on a medicine, do not stop taking it without a doctor’s permission -- If you feel as if you need help, reach out to a therapist or tell your doctor about your issues -- When having obsessive thoughts, do not question them as this will only worsen one’s OCD -- Do not do everything to perfection. In turn, this helps lessen one's OCD. -- Try mindfulness-based cognitive behavioral therapy to challenge the way you interpret your OCD

Disorder: Bi-Polar
Description: mental disorder that causes unusual and extreme shifts in mood, energy, activity levels, concentration, and the ability to carry out day-to-day tasks. These moods range from periods of extremely “up,” elated, irritable, or energized behavior (known as manic episodes) to very “down,” sad, indifferent, or hopeless periods (known as depressive episodes). 
Symptoms: Frequent and sporadic shifts in energy levels from high to low or vice versa -- Having inconsistent sleep patterns as a result of having numerous manic and depressive episode -- Losing appetite when having a manic episode and having a sudden appetite when in a depressive episode -- Having sudden bursts of confidence which are followed by depressive episodes
Tips: Eat healthy, well balanced meals -- Limit alcohol and drug use -- Get enough sleep -- Exercise for 30 minutes daily -- If you are on a medicine, do not stop taking it without a doctor’s permission -- If you feel as if you need help, reach out to a therapist or tell your doctor about your issues -- Try Psychotherapy to develop a treatment plan to identify triggers and change trouble emotions, thoughts, and behaviors -- In rare cases, Electroconvulsive Therapy (brain stimulation procedure) may help. It has helped people get relief from severe symptoms. -- Establish a healthy daily routine to prevent additional stressors from worsening one’s OCD -- Develop a plan for when you are suffering from episodes

Disorder: PTSD
Description: a disorder that develops in some people who have experienced a shocking, scary, or dangerous event. People who have PTSD may feel stressed or frightened, even when they are not in danger due to fear triggers related to their past experiences.
Symptoms: Having frequent flashbacks of a traumatic experience in the past. In these moments, one will likely be sweating, anxious, and have a high heart rate -- Having nightmares about the trauma / having difficulty sleeping -- Staying away from places, events, or objects that are reminders of the experience -- Having frequent outbursts of anger, or feeling “on edge” -- Distorted feelings like guilt or blame -- Loss of interest in enjoyable activities
Tips: Eat healthy, well balanced meals -- Limit alcohol and drug use -- Get enough sleep -- Exercise for 30 minutes daily -- If you are on a medicine, do not stop taking it without a doctor’s permission -- If you feel as if you need help, reach out to a therapist or tell your doctor about your issues -- Try Psychotherapy (either one-on-one with a therapist or with a group) -- Try exposure therapy to learn to healthily deal with triggers and episodes -- Try cognitive restructuring therapy -- Use relaxation techniques, such as counting to 10 slowly, when triggered

Disorder: Substance Abuse
Description: a disease that affects a person's brain and behavior and leads to an inability to control the use of a legal or illegal drug or medication. Substances such as alcohol, marijuana and nicotine also are considered drugs.
Symptoms: Feeling that you have to use the drug regularly — daily or even several times a day -- Using the drug to control emotions -- Building a tolerance to the drug -- Increasing the amount used overtime -- Making certain that you maintain a supply of the drug -- Spending money on the drug, even though you can't afford it -- Not meeting obligations and work responsibilities due to the drug -- Not going to social events or recreational activities because of the drug -- Unethically getting money for the drug, such as stealing -- Failing in your attempts to stop using the drug -- Experiencing withdrawal symptoms when you attempt to stop taking the drug
Tips: Eat healthy, well balanced meals -- Limit alcohol and drug use -- Get enough sleep -- Exercise for 30 minutes daily -- If you are on a medicine, do not stop taking it without a doctor’s permission -- If you feel as if you need help, reach out to a therapist or tell your doctor about your issues -- If you suddenly stop taking a particular drug and withdrawal symptoms are extreme, visit an Emergency room immediately -- Do an inpatient or outpatient detox -- If any other mental health issues, such as anxiety or depression, develop as a result of an eating disorder, make sure to get appropriate treatment -- Join support groups to help get over addiction

Disorder: Schizophrenia Treatment
Description: a chronic and severe mental disorder that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. People with schizophrenia may seem like they have lost touch with reality. -- Symptoms: Positive Symptoms [psychotic behaviors not generally seen in healthy people]: Having frequent hallucinations and delusions, having thought disorders, such as unusual or dysfunctional ways of thinking, and having movement disorders (agitated body movements) -- Negative Symptoms [associated with disruptions to normal emotions and behaviors]: having reduced feelings of pleasure in everyday life, difficulty beginning and sustaining activities, and reduced speaking -- Cognitive Symptoms: Poor “executive functioning” (the ability to understand information and use it to make decisions), trouble focusing or paying attention, and problems with “working memory” (the ability to use information immediately after learning it)
Tips: Eat healthy, well balanced meals -- Limit alcohol and drug use -- Get enough sleep -- Exercise for 30 minutes daily -- If you are on a medicine, do not stop taking it without a doctor’s permission -- If you feel as if you need help, reach out to a therapist or tell your doctor about your issues -- Ask your doctor if Antipsychotics would be good for you. They can help reduce symptoms. -- Get psychosocial treatments to address the everyday challenges of schizophrenia. Those who use such therapy tend to have less relapses. -- Get Coordinated Specialty Care to further help recovery

Disorder: Anorexia (Eating Disorder)
Description: An eating disorder characterized by markedly reduced appetite or total aversion to food. It is common with young girls or women who feel insecure about their weight, and therefore, avoid food.
Symptoms: extremely restricted eating -- emaciation -- having a relentless pursuit to be thin -- having an intense fear of gaining weight, and having a distorted body image influenced by one’s boy weight. Other symptoms include: thinning of bones, constant muscle fatigue, brittle hair/nails, severe constipation, low blood pressure, infertility, and a consistent low body temperature
Tips: Eat healthy, well balanced meals -- Limit alcohol and drug use -- Get enough sleep -- Exercise for 30 minutes daily [do not over do] -- If you are on a medicine, do not stop taking it without a doctor’s permission -- If you feel as if you need help, reach out to a therapist or tell your doctor about your issues -- Avoid internet communities which promote anorexia -- Join a support group with those who have suffered from an eating disorder -- Visit a nutritionist to set a good diet -- Adapt a more flexible diet -- Avoid counting calories -- If you find yourself extremely ill from excessive-dieting, visit an Emergency Room immediately -- Call the National Eating Disorders Asssociation's hotline at 1-800-931-2237
-----------
Disorder: Bulimia (Eating Disorder)
Description: An eating disorder characterized by episodes of secretive excessive eating (binge-eating) followed by inappropriate methods of weight control, such as self-induced vomiting (purging), abuse of laxatives and diuretics, or excessive exercise.
Symptoms: Chronically inflamed and sore throat -- swollen salivary glands in the neck and jaw area -- worn tooth enamel and increasingly sensitive and decaying teeth as a result of exposure to stomach acid -- acid reflux disorder and other gastrointestinal problems -- intestinal distress and irritation from laxative abuse -- severe dehydration, from purging of fluids -- electrolyte imbalance (too low or too high levels of sodium, calcium, potassium, and other minerals) which can lead to stroke or heart attack
Tips: Eat healthy, well balanced meals -- Limit alcohol and drug use -- Get enough sleep -- Exercise for 30 minutes daily -- If you are on a medicine, do not stop taking it without a doctor’s permission -- If you feel as if you need help, reach out to a therapist or tell your doctor about your issues -- Join a support group with those who have suffered from an eating disorder -- Visit a nutritionist to set a good diet -- Identify and remove any triggers which cause binge-eating to occur --Call the National Eating Disorders Asssociation's hotline at 1-800-931-2237
-----------
Disorder: Binge-Eating Disorder (Eating Disorder)
Description: An eating disorder in which you frequently consume unusually large amounts of food and feel unable to stop eating.
Symptoms: Eating unusually large amounts of food in a specific amount of time, such as a 2-hour period -- Eating even when you're full or not hungry -- Eating fast during binge episodes -- Eating until you're uncomfortably full -- Eating alone or in secret to avoid embarrassment -- feeling distressed, ashamed, or guilty about your eating -- frequently dieting, possibly without weight loss
Tips: Eat healthy, well balanced meals -- Limit alcohol and drug use -- Get enough sleep -- Exercise for 30 minutes daily -- If you are on a medicine, do not stop taking it without a doctor’s permission -- If you feel as if you need help, reach out to a therapist or tell your doctor about your issues -- Join a support group with those who have suffered from an eating disorder -- Visit a nutritionist to set a good diet -- Identify and remove any triggers which cause binge-eating to occur -- Count your calories to ensure that you are eating enough and not going overboard -- Call the National Eating Disorders Asssociation's hotline at 1-800-931-2237
